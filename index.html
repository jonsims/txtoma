Supercharger Route Card · Return Trip

Austin → Needham

Chargers chosen as destinations, not waypoints the car picks. Fewer, better stops — shade, seating off the ground, a walkable carry-out, and grass to walk Luna away from the crowd.

🚗 Model Y Long Range 3 days · Jul 9–11 ≈2,005 mi Christina + Luna 🐕 Plan legs ~230 mi

Last trip stacked up too many stops, each chosen for the fewest charging minutes, at exposed strip-mall chargers with nowhere good to be with a reactive dog in the July heat. This time the plan anchors each day on one genuinely pleasant place and keeps everything else short. Two overnight plugs shape the whole thing.

◐ Little Rock · Level 1 only

Arrive ~80%, wake at ~95%

A 120V plug adds just ~3–4 mi/hr → ~45–55 mi overnight. So Day 1's final stop tops up only enough to arrive ~80%. Chasing a true 100% would mean arriving ~85% — not worth it; ~95% covers Day 2's first leg easily.

⚡ Check, VA · Level 2

Arrive low, leave full

~25–30 mi/hr recovers a full charge overnight regardless of arrival state. No last-stop constraint on Day 2 — roll in low, leave Day 3 at 100%.

Collin Street Bakery · Waco, TX
100 mi in
Beat-the-heat lunch ⚡250 kW · 22 stalls

Fresh bakery/deli with an A/C dining room, free coffee for Tesla drivers, and dog treats on the shelf. Best food-plus-fast-charge on the corridor; the indoor cool is the July win. Use the far pedestals for full power. Store closes 6 PM.

Maps ↗ Bakery menu ↗
Downtown Square · Sulphur Springs, TX
280 mi in
★ Anchor · the stretch ⚡150 kW · 8 stalls

The only real town center on the route: Celebration Plaza with shaded bistro seating, Buford Park's pond and trees, and walkable carry-out (Corner Grub House, Pioneer Cafe, Arturo's). For Luna: walk her a block off the square on quiet side streets, away from the plaza crowd — flexibility a Buc-ee's can't give. 150 kW is fine since you're lingering anyway.

Casey Jones Village · Jackson, TN
224 mi in
★ Anchor · lunch ⚡250 kW · 24 stalls

The best stop of the whole trip — the Supercharger sits inside a historic village: Old Country Store buffet (carry-out capable), lawns and gardens, shaded walkways, a steam locomotive. Real perimeter grass to walk Luna away from foot traffic, and a long charge that overlaps lunch.

Jimmy John's / Taco Bell / Food Lion adjacent. Throttles to ~66 kW when stalls share — allow extra time. Lot-edge grass only; a relief stop, not a lounge.

Newer and less congested than old Turkey Creek. A 10-minute splash here removes all risk before the Virginia mountains. Skip it if range is healthy.

Fast, with on-site food and restrooms; Abingdon town has more walkable options. Charge enough for the final ~100–115 mi. Watch: a new Wytheville–Lithia Rd site (Cracker Barrel, 500 kW) is under construction — a big upgrade if it's live by July.

Overnight · Level 2

Arrive low and plug into the Airbnb's Level 2 to leave at 100%. Note: Christiansburg has no Tesla Supercharger — Wytheville (150 kW) is the last option, only if you'd otherwise land below ~15–20%.

The tradeoff · 3 vs 4 charges

Recommended — 4 charges: Jackson → Cookeville → Knoxville splash → Bristol/Abingdon. No leg over ~130 mi after Jackson; the splash de-risks the mountains and barely costs time since Jackson is a long lunch anyway.

Lean — 3 charges: drop Knoxville, but Cookeville→Bristol becomes ~215 mi through the mountains — leave Cookeville at 90%+ (a long session at a slow 150 kW site) and watch consumption.

Out of the Valley ⚡250 kW · 8 stalls

Sheetz Made-To-Order is the best fast-casual carry-out on the first half — touchscreen order, real food, fast. Lot-edge grass for a quick dog leg; a ~20–25 min V3 top-up is exactly the length of a food order and a walk.

Maps ↗
Clinton Station Diner · Clinton, NJ
410 mi in
★ Anchor · before the crunch ⚡250 kW · 12–15 stalls

An actual place, not a lot: a 1927 rail-car diner with a famous dessert case, sit-down or carry-out — and it lands right before the I-287 / Tappan Zee / NYC crunch. For Luna: historic downtown Clinton (the Red Mill and the South Branch Raritan riverside walk) is ~1 mi away — quiet grass and water, away from crowds.

Sturbridge Plaza · Sturbridge, MA
600 mi in
The post-traffic exhale ⚡250 kW · 12 stalls

After Danbury, Waterbury and Hartford: a pleasant New England town at the I-84 / Mass Pike seam with plaza restaurants, Dunkin, and real greenspace (town common, Old Sturbridge Village grounds) for a proper dog walk. Only ~55 mi from home — charge just enough to finish, keep it short.

The tradeoff · 3 vs 4 charges

Recommended — 3 charges: Winchester → Clinton → Sturbridge. Spacing 215 / 195 / 190 / 55 mi — every leg under 230 with buffer, all three 250 kW V3. Clean.

Conservative — add a 4th: a short splash at Southington, CT (Target, 250 kW) splits the Clinton→Sturbridge leg if the hilly, traffic-heavy CT stretch leaves you wanting cushion. Fast insurance, not a relax stop — save that for Sturbridge.
